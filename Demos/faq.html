CITL AI Fluency Certificate – Frequently Asked Questions

What is the AI Fluency Certificate?

CITL offers a flexible certifcate program that teaches foundational AI concepts, effective collaboration strategies, customization techniques, and multimodal skills. Learners complete four core modules plus two electives, combining asynchronous content with optional workshops. You can self-enroll in the program here.

Who can enroll?

The certificate is open to UIUC students, faculty, and staff with a NetID.  No prior AI or technical background is required.

Can I complete everything asynchronously?

Yes. All required components can be completed online at your own pace. Live virtual workshops are offered for those who prefer learning in this format.

Do I need technical or coding experience?

No. The modules are designed for beginners and focus on concepts, workflows, and applied practice rather than programming.

How long does the certificate take to complete?

Most participants finish in 12-20 hours total, depending on elective choices and workshop participation. Each core module is roughly 90-minutes worth of content and guided activities.    

What are the required modules?

You must complete all four core modules:

  • Understanding What GenAI Is — and Isn’t
  • Collaboration Iteration with GenAI
  • Customizing AI for Consistency
  • Engaging with Multimodal GenAI

How do electives work?

You choose two electives aligned with your interests. Each elective requires a minimum of 60 minutes of engagement and a short reflection or artifact. Electives may include workshops, campus AI events, discipline-specific sessions, or external certifications

Is the certificate graded?

No traditional grades are assigned. You complete small activities, quizzes and  reflections to demonstrate understanding.

Do I receive a digital badge or certificate?

Yes. Upon completion, you receive a verified digital certificate suitable for LinkedIn or professional portfolios.

What AI tools will I use?

You'll gain hands-on exposure to a broad ecosystem such as Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Adobe Firefly, NotebookLM, and Illinois Chat (our secure, campus-hosted model). Tool availability may evolve as technologies change.

Is there a cost?

No. The certificate is free for UIUC students, faculty, and staff.

Do modules have to be taken in order?

It is best to complete the modules in sequence, but it is not a requirement. 

What kinds of activities will I complete?

Interactive demos, structured prompts, scenario-based decision-making, multimodal critiques, and small applied artifacts tied to your work or studies.

Do I need to purchase software?

No. All required tools have free tiers or are campus-provided.

Can I use this certificate as an elective for my academic program?

No. This is strictly a co-curricular or professional development opportunity, although some courses may provide extra credit for completion.

Does it appear on my academic transcript?

No. It is generally a co-curricular credential. Some units may record it in professional development tracking systems.

Can faculty integrate training into their courses?

Yes. Faculty are encouraged to provide a self-enrollment link to the Certificate Program in Canvas. Guidance for adaptation is available on request.

What support is available?

CITL offers AI consulting as well as workshops and drop-in sessions to provide additional help as needed.

Will this certify me to teach AI?

Not formally, but it provides sufficient grounding to use AI responsibly and guide others in foundational skills.
